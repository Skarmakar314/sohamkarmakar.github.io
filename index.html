About Me

I am an independent researcher with an inquisitive mind, fascinated by the subject matters of mathematics, computer science, and the unseen links between different forms of knowledge. My research focuses on patterns in prime numbers, theoretical systems, and the prospect that music, logic, and nature are all governed by some underlying principles of mathematics. I find myself fascinated by the prospect of learning how complex concepts may be built upon simple principles, and how abstract concepts may appear in the world around us. Along with research, I have a strong passion for Indian classical music, portrait sketching, and photography. I think these activities train the mind to observe and find beauty in details, balance, and expression. Be it music, the human face, or mathematical formulas, I find beauty in all these forms. I think beauty is not just limited to the realm of art; it is in numbers, patterns, nature, and life itself. I am also interested in the philosophy and psychology of man, particularly in the notion that thoughts, emotions, and behaviors may be governed by mathematical principles. The exploration of the human mind in terms of logic, symmetry, and patterns is perhaps one of the most intriguing research problems to me, as it links the realm of science with the realm of experience, reason with the realm of imagination. Research, to me, is not just a profession but a journey, and I would like to continue this journey of study, thinking, and discovery throughout my life. The more one delves deeper into research, the more it feels like entering a fractal, where each answer leads to another, each layer leads to another layer, and so on, with no end in sight. In this journey, perhaps the researcher becomes disconnected from the world outside, not because he is disconnected from it, but because he is now infinitely connected to the world inside. Perhaps this is the beauty of knowledge: the more one knows, the more he realizes that there is no end to what he may find, no last discovery to be made, just an endless journey waiting to be traversed.
